How this demo works

This is a live, in-browser neural network. There is no server and no simulation running — prediction is instant.

The model. A multilayer perceptron (33 → 64 → 32 → 16 → 1) trained offline on 495,984 real building records from the research dataset. On a held-out 20% test split it reaches R² = 0.95 with RMSE ≈ 0.81 (MAE ≈ 0.55), consistent with the published thesis results. The trained weights are embedded in this page and the forward pass runs in plain JavaScript.

The inputs. Each building is described exactly as in the research framework: its area, height, block and building orientation, street and urban-void exposure, edges and court, and — crucially — its surrounding context: for each of the 8 compass directions, how many neighbours are higher, equal, or lower. When you raise a building, its neighbours' context changes, so their predictions change too.

What it is not. The output approximates full environmental simulation; it is a fast surrogate, not a replacement for EnergyPlus / Ladybug. Shading is captured through the neighbour-height relationships the model was trained on, not ray-traced. Street generation here is a procedural stand-in for the full parametric framework.
